# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sebastián — a delivery/technical executive at DFX5 running many concurrent client
projects across LATAM (Seguros Bolívar, Belcorp, Itaú, Grupo Aval…). His day is
back-to-back meetings captured on a HiDock USB recorder, plus Outlook calendar,
Slack, and repos on disk. Context when using the app: between meetings or first
thing in the morning, asking "what happened, what do I owe, what's next?" He is
technical and impatient with busywork; he expects the app to act, not to present
lists.

## Product Purpose

An assistant for daily work, not a recordings manager. It transcribes everything
the HiDock records automatically, builds a central knowledge base (transcripts,
summaries, people, projects, action items, embeddings, plain-markdown wiki),
correlates recordings with calendar meetings, and proactively proposes follow-ups
(draft docs, Claude Code handoffs, meeting minutes, syncs to schedule). Success:
months later he can ask about any project and pick up where he left off; after a
call the app tells him what to do next; the knowledge is equally usable by
external agents (Claude Code) through plain files.

## Brand Personality

Calm intelligence. Proactive but never noisy. Trustworthy with his data.
Three words: anticipatory, grounded, unified (one integrated surface like Office
365 apps — including the window titlebar merged with the app chrome).

## Anti-references

- "A list of recordings with dates" — the app's own previous state; explicitly
  rejected by the user.
- File-manager UX: browsing, statuses, filenames as the primary object.
- Generic SaaS dashboard with hero metrics and identical card grids.
- Floating native titlebar disconnected from the app (pre-unification look).
- Flat, all-white, two-color surfaces with no depth (user's explicit feedback:
  "too flat in white backgrounds everywhere... no shadows, only 2 colors").

## Design Principles

1. Assistant-first: every screen answers "what should I do next?", not "what
   files exist?". Today is the front door.
2. Knowledge compounds: every recording enriches people, projects, actions, and
   memory automatically — zero manual filing.
3. Actions over data: surfaces lead with one-click follow-ups (generate, hand
   off, schedule), data sits behind them.
4. One unified surface: app chrome, titlebar, and content feel like a single
   integrated desktop app (Office 365 style), not a web page in a frame.
5. Plain-files interop: everything the app knows is exportable/readable as
   markdown for humans and coding agents.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target (per repo docs): keyboard navigation, screen-reader labels,
reduced-motion alternatives. Spanish/English bilingual content is first-class —
transcripts and generated outputs follow the meeting's language.
